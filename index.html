About me

I’ve always been excited to start university and now that I’m here it’s such a strange feeling. Getting used to uni-life is going to be a major transition in my life. My name is Nik I’m 18 years old, I have two brothers one older and the other younger. We’ve been in Melbourne my entire life. Although I come from Australia and was born here my father’s is Hungarian. I have a part time job working at our local pizza shop where I manage the phone calls, the restaurant, front counter and online orders. When I’m not working, I’m enjoying gaming, hanging out with friends, cycling and watching movies/TV Shows some of my favorites are Blade runner (Both the original and sequel), Arrival, Alien and spirited away. As you can tell from this list, I like sci-fi, in this genre development of computer science is explored in a fantasy setting and analyzed through characters perspective of the world. One of my favorite books is A Brave New World by Aldous Huxley. This book explores a technologically advanced utopian version of the world we live in today, this is very much the opposite of most sci-fi films based on a dystopian world. Watching and reading this media opened my mind to all the possibilities for the future of technology. However, my interest in IT started before I was introduced into the sci-fi genre. I’ve been interested in IT since I was very little, involving myself with the computer whenever I could. I would explore windows and fiddle with every little setting. I remember when I was little kid I somehow managed to open all exe files with notepad and 10-year-old me was trying so hard to find a fix to it before my dad used the computer knowing that he would not let me back on the computer after messing it up. I can’t remember if I ever found the fix for it, but it stopped me from playing some of my favorite computer games. Well I must have survived without them because I’m here now studying at RMIT. I’m going to be honest RMIT was not my first preference, but it was still amazing that I got accepted into the course.
